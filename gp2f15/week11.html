PROJECT 2: PLATFORMER, NARRATIVE (DUE 12/16)

For the final project, you will be responsible for completing a small 2D game which uses platformer mechanics to tell a story. This narrative can be specific, atmospheric, linear, or non-linear. It can be an original story, fiction, non-fiction, autobiographical, or based on some other pre-existing (non-game, non-film) source(s). Lastly, while text can be used to establish the narrative elements, it must also be conveyed somehow through the game's mechanics, dynamics, and aesthetics.

As with the last project, everyone will work in small groups to design, prototype, and develop a single completed game which will be presented on the last day of class (12/16). The workload must be shared equally between all team members. It will be your collective responsibility to delegate tasks (who will code what parts, who will handle visual assets, who will be responsible for sound, etc.) At the end of the project, each team member will responsible for completnig a peer review to provide feedback on his/her partners.

REQUIREMENTS

  • One player only.
  • Focused on telling a story.
  • Multiple highly polished levels ok, though I'd recommend sticking to one, if possible.
  • Game must have an end condition.
  • Controls limited to: directional movement controlled by a joystick/gamepad, 2 input buttons, 1 start button (no more! no mouse!)
  • Must include the following scenes (at minimum): Start, Game, End
  • Complete instructions somehow include in-game.
  • A Cohesive and memorable concept established through sound, visuals, and animation.
  • Unique assets (no unlicensed sound or visuals)
